DAY 37 SUMMARY — Risk Management & Security Regulations
1. Risk Management (Video 5.2)
What Is Risk?

Risk is the likelihood of uncertainty becoming an issue.

Organizations must:

Identify risks

Evaluate risks

Prioritize risks

Plan how to handle them before they escalate

Risk Assessments

Performed regularly to understand exposure.

Not all risk needs constant evaluation — some risks require:

One-time assessment

Continuous monitoring

Ad-hoc checks

Types:

Continuous: part of operations; new vulnerabilities constantly discovered

Ad-hoc: unplanned assessments triggered by events or concerns

Periodic: quarterly, annual, etc.

Mandatory: required by law or compliance frameworks

Compliance-Driven Risk Assessment

Some industries legally require formal assessments.

PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) requires yearly risk assessments.

Other orgs may require:

Every 3 months

At the start of new projects

When implementing new technology

Risk assessments help companies determine:

What assets are valuable

What threats exist

What controls already protect them

What additional controls are required

2. Security Considerations & Compliance (Video 5.1)
Regulations

Many security responsibilities are mandated by law, including:

Security controls

Reporting requirements

Breach notifications

Examples:

HIPAA (US healthcare) — strict handling of medical information

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley) — financial accountability

GDPR (Europe) — data privacy & control for individuals

GLBA — banking & finance

Industry-Specific Requirements

Each industry may have:

Mandatory encryption laws

Data handling rules

Audit requirements

Reporting timelines

Global, Federal, and Local Regulatory Layers

Multi-national organizations must comply with:

Different states

Different countries

Federal-level rules

This complicates:

Cloud storage

Data residency laws

Cross-border data sharing

3. Data Roles & Responsibilities
Data Owner

High-level authority over the data

Typically a senior leader

Responsible for:

Classifying data

Specifying controls

Data Controller

Manages how data is processed

Defines exactly which data is collected and how it is used

Data Processor

Processes data on behalf of the controller

Often 3rd-party companies

Example: Payroll companies processing employee salary data

Data Custodian

Responsible for:

Storing data safely

Managing security controls

Maintaining backups

Protecting confidentiality & integrity
