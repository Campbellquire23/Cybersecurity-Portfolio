DAY 41 SUMMARY — Assessment Types, Agreements, and Compliance
1. Assessment Types (Video 5.3)
Common Assessments

Used to evaluate:

Security posture

Operational effectiveness

Risk exposure

Often performed:

Internally

By third parties

On a scheduled or event-driven basis

Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

Define minimum service expectations

Common elements:

Uptime guarantees

Response times

Performance metrics

Frequently used with:

Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

Cloud providers

Managed service vendors

2. Memorandums & Agreements
Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)

Describes intent and cooperation

Usually:

Non-binding

High-level

Common in:

Partnerships

Government or inter-org cooperation

Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)

More formal than an MOU

Often:

Legally binding

Defines responsibilities

May exist even without payment involved

3. Master & Work Agreements
Master Service Agreement (MSA)

Broad framework contract

Covers:

Legal terms

Responsibilities

Governance

Future work is built on top of the MSA

Statement of Work (SOW) / Work Order

Specific tasks tied to the MSA

Includes:

Scope of work

Deliverables

Timelines

Acceptance criteria

Used to answer:

“Was the job done correctly?”

4. Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)

Protect confidential information

Can be:

Unilateral

Bilateral

Multilateral

Types:

One-way NDAs

Mutual NDAs

Usually require formal signatures

Protect:

Trade secrets

Business strategies

Sensitive technical information

5. Business Partnership Agreements (BPAs)

Define:

Ownership stakes

Financial responsibilities

Decision-making authority

May include:

Contingency plans

Asset recovery

Financial risk handling

Used when organizations operate closely together

✅ 6. Compliance (Video 5.4)
What Is Compliance?

Adherence to:

Laws

Regulations

Policies

Industry standards

Requires:

Continuous monitoring

Documentation

Auditing

7. Regulatory Requirements & Consequences
Major Regulations Covered

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)

Financial accountability for public companies

HIPAA

Protection of healthcare information

GLBA

Safeguards customer financial data

Non-Compliance Risks

Fines

Sanctions

Civil penalties

Criminal penalties (in severe cases)

Reputational damage

Loss of licenses

Contract termination

8. Reputational & Contractual Impact

Damage to trust can:

Reduce customers

Impact partnerships

Affect revenue

Some organizations:

Will not do business with non-compliant companies

Contracts may:

Be revoked

Be invalidated due to compliance failures

9. Compliance Monitoring & Due Diligence

Ongoing process, not one-time

Includes:

Internal audits

External audits

Continuous assessment

Due diligence:

Acting in good faith

Proactively identifying and fixing issues

10. Attestation & Accountability

Attestation:

Formal acknowledgment of compliance

Requires signatures

Signers are personally accountable if documentation is incorrect

Applies to:

Internal staff

Third parties

Vendors

11. Automation & Scale

Large organizations rely on:

Automated compliance tools

Monitoring systems

Automation helps:

Collect data

Generate reports

Track compliance across regions and vendors
